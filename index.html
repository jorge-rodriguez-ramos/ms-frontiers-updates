$InvOLS_{free,SHO}$¶

$$ \frac{1}{B} \left( \frac{k_B T \,2Q} { k_1 \, \pi \, f_r} \right)^{1/2}$$
In [3]:
sho = 1/B *( kB * T * 2*Q / (k1 * p  *fr) )**(1/2)
print('InvOLS_SHO')
N(sho,5)

InvOLS_SHO
Out[3]:
$\displaystyle \frac{1.4142 \left(\frac{Q T k_{B}}{f_{r} k_{1} \pi}\right)^{0.5}}{B}$
In [4]:
pirzer = 1/B *( kB * T * 2*Q / (k1 * p  *fr *( p/2 + atan(2*Q))) )**(1/2)
print('InvOLS_Pirzer')
N(pirzer,5)

InvOLS_Pirzer
Out[4]:
$\displaystyle \frac{1.4142 \left(\frac{Q T k_{B}}{f_{r} k_{1} \pi \left(0.5 \pi + \operatorname{atan}{\left(2 Q \right)}\right)}\right)^{0.5}}{B}$

error propagation¶

In the first approach, I only considered B and Q are the main sources of error. So I will do the propagation for them only. I will consider B nd Q are independent among each other. k_c is not independent, but I will assume (in a first approach), that it is not asimportant as B and Q.

The new approach includes fr, for the sake of comparison

SD SHO¶

In [5]:
Bterm_sho = (sho.diff(B)*dB)**2 
Qterm_sho = (sho.diff(Q)*dQ)**2
frterm_sho = (sho.diff(fr)*dfr)**2
sd_sho = simplify(( Bterm_sho + Qterm_sho + frterm_sho )**.5)
N(sd_sho,5)
Out[5]:
$\displaystyle 1.4142 \left(\frac{\left(\frac{Q T k_{B}}{f_{r} k_{1} \pi}\right)^{1.0} \left(0.25 B^{2} Q^{2} dfr^{2} + 0.25 B^{2} \delta_{Q}^{2} f_{r}^{2} + Q^{2} \delta_{B}^{2} f_{r}^{2}\right)}{B^{4} Q^{2} f_{r}^{2}}\right)^{0.5}$
